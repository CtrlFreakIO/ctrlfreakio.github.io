# About Me

I am an automation engineer and researcher who enjoys solving problems that sit at the 
boundary of disciplines — where hardware meets software, and where engineering practice 
meets formal methods. I work at Poznań University of Technology, but my roots are firmly 
in industry.

---

## Engineering Experience

With over 8 years of hands-on experience, I have been involved in industrial projects 
ranging from single machines to complete production lines. My work spans the full 
engineering stack:

**Hardware & Control Systems**
From large-scale hydraulic presses to AGV systems and process installations — I design, 
program, and commission control systems using Siemens (TIA Portal, Step 7) and Rockwell 
(Studio5000) platforms, including safety controllers.

**Machine Safety**
I design machine safety systems in accordance with the Machinery Directive and harmonized 
standards (EN ISO 13849-1, IEC 62061). I conduct safety audits and develop safety concepts 
for both new machines and modernizations.

**IT/OT Integration**
Beyond the PLC layer, I connect operational technology with information systems. I develop 
tools that extract data from PLC controllers and route it toward storage and server-side 
processing — bridging the gap between the shop floor and the data layer. I also work with 
SQL-based data migration between industrial servers.

**Visualization & SCADA**
I design HMI visualizations, SCADA systems, and MES integrations for production 
environments.

## Research

My research focuses on **automating functional safety engineering** for industrial control 
systems — an area where formal rigor meets everyday engineering practice.

**Graph-Based Safety Category Assessment**
I develop graph-theoretic methods for preliminary safety category classification under 
**EN ISO 13849-1**, transforming electrical schematic data into directed graph models for 
automated architectural analysis. The goal is to reduce manual effort and improve 
consistency in safety pre-assessment workflows.

**Formal Verification of Normative Safety Requirements**
I investigate the translation of natural-language safety norms (ISO 13850, ISO 3691-4) 
into verifiable control logic using a multi-layer pipeline: **FRET** for normative 
consistency, **NuSMV** for behavioral verification, and **UPPAAL** for real-time 
constraint checking.

Future directions include the revised **Machinery Regulation (EU) 2023/1230** and the 
role of artificial intelligence in machine safety systems.
